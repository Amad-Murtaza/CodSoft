Sayyid Abul A'la al-Maududi

Life of a prominent Islamic scholar, theologian, and political thinker.

Early Life

Sayyid Abul A'la al-Maududi,also known as Molana Maududi was born on september 25,1903, in Aurangabad, Bitish India. He was the youngest son of Ahmad Hasan.His father was a lawyer by profession.

Education

Maududi received his early education in British India. He was proficient in Arabic, Persian, and Urdu, which are essential languages for the study of Islamic texts He furthered his education in Islamic studies by studying various Islamic sciences, including Tafsir (Quranic exegesis), Hadith (Prophet Muhammad's sayings and actions), and Fiqh (Islamic jurisprudence). He studied under the guidance of various scholars, which provided him with a strong foundation in traditional Islamic knowledge.In addition to his traditional Islamic studies, Maududi also pursued modern education, including subjects like philosophy and history. This diverse educational background allowed him to engage with contemporary issues from both an Islamic and a secular perspective.

Political Party

Maulana Abul Ala Maududi was the founder of Jamaat-e-Islami, a prominent Islamist political and social organization in South Asia. Jamaat-e-Islami was founded in 1941 in British India, with the goal of advocating for the implementation of Islamic principles and the establishment of an Islamic state based on Sharia law.

Ideology

Maulana Abul Ala Maududi's ideology, often referred to as Maududism or Maududi's thought, is rooted in his interpretation of Islam and his vision for an Islamic state. His ideology has had a significant impact on Islamic political thought, particularly in South Asia.Maududi ideology.Maududi believed in the establishment of an Islamic state governed by Sharia law.Maududi's vision of an Islamic state was comprehensive, and he advocated for an all-encompassing role of religion in public life.

Death

Maulana Abul Ala Maududi, the founder of Jamaat-e-Islami and a prominent Islamic scholar and thinker, passed away on September 22, 1979. His death occurred in the city of Buffalo, New York, in the United States, where he had been living for a period of time before his passing.
